## Sourcing Notes

### Silicone wire

BNTechgo silicone wire is the community standard for QRP portable. 28 AWG is the right balance of weight, flexibility, and durability. Avoid:

- PTFE / Teflon (stiffer, kinks)
- Bare enamel magnet wire (no insulation if it nicks)
- PVC-jacketed (memory-effect tangles in cold)

Hi-vis colors matter for finding the wire end in grass. Orange, hi-vis green, or yellow are all good. Black is the worst color for a portable kit.

### Spade lugs

Match the slot width to the KH1 ground stud size:
- KH1 stud: 4-40 thread, ~3 mm OD
- Spade slot: 3 mm (M3 / #4 in different naming conventions)

Insulated spades (with built-in vinyl jacket) are easier than bare spades, but bare ones with heat-shrink are lighter and less bulky. Either works.

### Banana hardware

For the in-line plug and socket on Wires A and B, Pomona 1325 series (low-profile, solder-on) or Mueller equivalents are reliable. Generic Amazon parts work for QRP power but can have inconsistent fit.

The plug and socket should mate firmly — a snug fit, not loose. Test with a pair before building the wires into them, since you're committing the wire to the part.
